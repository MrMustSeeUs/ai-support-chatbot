FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS:

Q: How do I get started?
A: Fill out the contact form at teocallidevs.com with a description of
your project. We will reach out within 1 business day to schedule a
discovery call.

Q: How much does a project cost?
A: Every project is scoped individually. We do not publish fixed rates
because we want to give you an accurate number based on your actual needs.
Contact us and we will send you a clear, no-obligation proposal.

Q: How long does a project take?
A: Timelines vary by project size. A focused MVP or AI integration can
take 2 to 6 weeks. Larger SaaS builds or cloud infrastructure projects
typically run 2 to 4 months. We will give you a realistic timeline in
your proposal.

Q: Do you work with clients outside the United States?
A: Yes. We work with clients globally. All communication is handled
remotely via video call, email, and project management tools.

Q: Can you work with our existing codebase?
A: Absolutely. We regularly step into existing projects to add features,
refactor code, integrate AI, or improve infrastructure. We will review
what you have before scoping the work.

Q: Do you offer ongoing support after launch?
A: Yes. We offer post-launch support and retainer arrangements for clients
who want continued development, maintenance, or monitoring. Ask us about
this during your consultation.

Q: What makes Teocalli Devs different?
A: We are engineers first, not a sales organization. Every client works
directly with senior-level developers — not account managers handing work
off to junior teams. We are also deeply invested in AI and cloud-native
development, which means we are building with the tools that matter most
right now.

Q: I am not sure what I need — can you still help?
A: Yes. That is exactly what our consulting engagements are for. Reach out,
tell us the problem you are trying to solve, and we will help you figure
out the right path forward.

Q: Can you sign an NDA?
A: Yes. We are happy to sign a mutual NDA before any sensitive project
details are shared. Just mention it when you reach out.
